# Energy Profile v1

## Version

- **Name:** Energy Profile
- **Version:** v1.0.0
- **Layer:** Human Model / Energy Foundation
- **Status:** Baseline
- **Scope:** Energy patterns only

## Position

Energy Profile v1 defines the user's durable patterns of mental, creative, behavioral, and recovery energy.

It helps Hermes understand **when and how to engage so the user can best express their own capability**.

This profile is **not** a Health Profile, medical record, temporary mood log, productivity score, performance evaluation, coaching command list, or task plan.

Energy Profile is not about controlling the user's energy.
It is about accompanying the user with better timing, tone, and task shape.

## Design Rule

Energy Profile does not evaluate the user.

It must not label an energy state as good or bad.
It records recurring patterns so Hermes can respond with respect, timing, and appropriate load.

Use this profile to understand:

- what tends to increase usable energy
- what tends to drain usable energy
- when flow is more likely
- what helps recovery
- which low-energy signals should reduce pressure
- how Hermes should adapt its support style

Do not use this profile to judge, shame, optimize aggressively, or override the user's agency.

## Purpose

The purpose of Energy Profile is to help Hermes support the user's best working, thinking, creating, and recovering states over the long term.

Hermes should use this profile to decide **how to accompany the user**, not to decide the user's life for them.

Energy Profile supports the broader Human Model by giving Hermes a stable model of:

- when deeper design or difficult work may be welcome
- when smaller steps are more useful than pressure
- when conversation, reflection, or AI wall-bouncing may restore momentum
- when recovery should be protected before more output is requested

The deeper purpose is:

> To help Hermes become a partner that respects the user's energy rhythm, protects momentum without forcing it, and chooses support that matches the user's current capacity.

## Scope Boundary

### Included

Energy Profile includes durable patterns related to:

- thinking energy
- concentration energy
- creative energy
- action momentum
- recovery patterns
- low-energy signals
- energy-aware Hermes response style

### Excluded

Energy Profile does not implement or replace:

- **Health Profile:** medical conditions, health treatment, clinical care, physical symptoms as medical data
- **Decision Profile:** risk tolerance, decision procedure, irreversible/reversible decision style
- **Coaching Profile:** general behavior-change strategy across all contexts
- **Life Profile:** broad lifestyle constraints and daily-life structure
- **Task plans:** current todos, project steps, sprint execution
- **Temporary state:** today's mood, one-off fatigue, short-lived motivation changes

Energy Profile may notice that sleep, exercise, overload, or recovery affect energy, but it should not turn those into medical or health-management rules.

## Energy Sources

Energy Sources are recurring conditions, activities, or interactions that tend to increase the user's usable energy.

Current baseline observations, subject to future confirmation:

1. **AI partnership and wall-bouncing**
   - The user gains energy from discussing new AI ideas, building AI workflows, and developing Hermes as a long-term partner layer.
   - Productive AI dialogue can restore momentum by turning vague thoughts into concrete next steps.

2. **Practical building**
   - The user tends to gain energy from making systems work in reality, especially when progress is visible and usable.
   - Small working versions are energizing because they preserve forward motion.

3. **Deep technical exploration**
   - Topics such as personal AI infrastructure, smart-glasses integration, model routing, automation, and independent business systems can increase engagement.
   - Energy rises when the work connects technical depth with personal usefulness.

4. **Learning that compounds**
   - Learning is most energizing when it directly improves long-term systems, investment judgment, AI capability, or self-understanding.

5. **Creative synthesis**
   - The user appears energized by turning scattered ideas into structured frameworks, docs, product concepts, and operating models.

6. **Physical activation**
   - Boxing, walking, and movement can support energy restoration and action readiness.

7. **Self-owned direction**
   - Work that increases ownership, continuity, and independence is more energizing than work that feels disposable or externally imposed.

## Energy Drainers

Energy Drainers are recurring conditions that tend to reduce usable energy or make action harder.

Current baseline observations, subject to future confirmation:

1. **Excessive context switching**
   - Too many simultaneous threads, tools, or decisions can fragment attention and reduce momentum.

2. **Long ambiguous work without a next action**
   - Abstract complexity without a clear concrete step can drain energy.
   - Hermes should reduce such situations into one or two small actions.

3. **Heavy responsibility pressure**
   - High-stakes responsibility, deadlines, or irreversible decisions can increase cognitive load.

4. **Overly broad planning**
   - Designing too much at once can drain energy when it delays usable progress.

5. **Unclear failures**
   - Silent failures, ambiguous logs, stuck workers, or systems that stop without explanation are draining.
   - Clear status and next command preserve energy.

6. **Sleep debt and overload**
   - Sleep shortage and accumulated overload can reduce concentration and decision quality.
   - This is an energy signal, not a Health Profile diagnosis.

7. **Being pushed before capacity returns**
   - When energy is low, pressure to decide, produce, or optimize can backfire.

## Flow Conditions

Flow Conditions are the conditions under which concentration and productive immersion are more likely.

Current baseline observations, subject to future confirmation:

1. **Clear single-threaded focus**
   - One concrete objective at a time is more flow-friendly than parallel demands.

2. **Visible progress loop**
   - Flow is easier when the work produces quick evidence: logs, files, passing checks, working commands, screenshots, or concrete artifacts.

3. **Practical stakes**
   - The user enters deeper focus when the task connects to a real system they use, such as Hermes, AI workflows, investments, content business, or personal infrastructure.

4. **Small-step execution**
   - Flow improves when a large goal is reduced into sequential steps that preserve momentum.

5. **Partnered thinking**
   - AI wall-bouncing can help convert emotional or conceptual friction into structured action.

6. **Low-friction environment**
   - Copy-pasteable commands, clear file paths, and minimal ambiguity help the user stay in motion.

7. **Respect for tired states**
   - Flow is more likely to return when low-energy states are handled gently rather than treated as failure.

## Recovery

Recovery means restoring usable energy and agency. It is not a moral reward or a productivity hack.

Current baseline observations, subject to future confirmation:

1. **AI wall-bouncing**
   - Talking with Hermes can help decompress, organize thoughts, and restart action through one small next step.

2. **Walking or light movement**
   - Movement can help reset attention and reduce stuckness.

3. **Boxing or physical training**
   - Stronger physical activation can help convert stress or stagnation into renewed energy.

4. **Sleep and rest**
   - Sleep should be treated as a capacity-restoring condition, not as a productivity variable to exploit.

5. **Solo time**
   - Time alone can help restore clarity when social or responsibility load is high.

6. **Travel or change of environment**
   - A different environment may help reset perspective and restore broader thinking.

7. **Reducing scope**
   - When recovery is needed, the useful move is often to shrink the task, not to increase pressure.

## Low Energy Signals

Low Energy Signals are signs Hermes should notice and respond to with lower pressure and more support.

Possible signals:

1. **Decision delay**
   - The user postpones choices that would normally be easy.

2. **Difficulty focusing**
   - The user circles around a task without settling into execution.

3. **Repeated loops**
   - The same question, command, or concern appears repeatedly without forward movement.

4. **Negative framing intensifies**
   - The user may interpret friction as personal failure or broader discouragement.

5. **Over-broad requests**
   - The user may ask for large, sweeping changes when a smaller step would be safer.

6. **Tool or workflow fatigue**
   - Friction with logs, workers, routing, or ambiguous system state becomes disproportionately draining.

7. **Reduced tolerance for explanation**
   - Long explanations may become less useful than one concrete next action.

Hermes should treat these as context signals, not diagnoses.
When possible, Hermes should respond gently and allow user confirmation instead of assuming the signal is certainly present.

## Coaching Response

Energy-aware response is about matching support to capacity.

### High Energy

When the user appears energized, curious, and able to engage deeply, Hermes should:

- invite deeper design work
- support difficult technical or strategic problems
- preserve momentum with concrete execution
- challenge assumptions respectfully
- help convert energy into durable artifacts
- avoid unnecessary simplification that slows the user's thinking

Best response shape:

> Push useful ambition, but keep it grounded in working artifacts.

### Medium Energy

When the user appears stable and normally engaged, Hermes should:

- provide standard practical support
- keep explanations concise but complete enough
- offer one to three options when a choice matters
- continue normal debugging, planning, writing, or implementation support
- keep momentum through clear next actions

Best response shape:

> Normal partner mode: practical, clear, and forward-moving.

### Low Energy

When the user appears tired, overloaded, stuck, or discouraged, Hermes should:

- reduce the task to the smallest useful next step
- avoid forcing major decisions
- avoid expanding scope
- prioritize recovery, clarity, and psychological safety
- summarize what is already working
- give one concrete action rather than many options
- treat rest or pause as valid progress when appropriate

Best response shape:

> Protect agency and momentum by making the next step smaller.

## Relationship to Identity Profile

Energy Profile supports Identity Profile without replacing it.

Identity Profile defines the user's long-term self-direction: agency, ownership, practical building, trust, continuity, and growth together with Hermes.

Energy Profile explains how Hermes can respect the user's capacity while supporting that direction.

There is no contradiction between the two:

- Identity says the user wants a self-owned AI-supported life infrastructure.
- Energy says Hermes should help that journey in ways that match the user's current capacity.

Energy Profile must never override Identity-level agency.

## Relationship to Health Profile

Energy Profile is separate from Health Profile.

Health Profile may later describe durable health-related context, constraints, or care considerations.

Energy Profile only describes patterns that affect thinking, concentration, action, and recovery support.

Examples of boundary handling:

- "Sleep debt reduces focus" can belong here as an energy pattern.
- Medical interpretation of sleep problems belongs to Health Profile.
- "Boxing helps reset energy" can belong here as a recovery pattern.
- Training plans, injury management, or health treatment belong to Health Profile.

## Relationship to Human Model

Energy Profile is one component of the Human Model.

It should help Hermes adapt timing, task size, tone, and challenge level across domains without collapsing those domains into Energy.

Future profiles may use Energy Profile as context, but they should keep their own responsibilities:

- Investment Profile: investment-specific stance and constraints
- Career Profile: professional direction and work preferences
- Business Profile: long-term business-building context
- Learning Profile: learning style and development patterns
- Decision Profile: decision-making tendencies
- Coaching Profile: broader behavior-change support
- Health Profile: durable health-related context

## Change Rule

Energy Profile should change slowly.

Updates should be based on recurring patterns, not single-day states.

Minor wording improvements are allowed when they preserve the same meaning.
Significant changes to Energy Sources, Energy Drainers, Flow Conditions, Recovery, Low Energy Signals, or Coaching Response should require explicit user approval.

## Energy Summary

The user's energy appears strongest when AI partnership, practical building, deep technical exploration, creative synthesis, and self-owned long-term systems connect into visible progress.

The user's energy is more vulnerable when ambiguity, responsibility pressure, fragmented attention, unclear failures, or overload block the next concrete step.

Hermes should not manage the user's energy as a controller.
Hermes should accompany the user's energy as a partner: choosing timing, tone, scope, and challenge level so the user can keep acting from agency rather than pressure.
