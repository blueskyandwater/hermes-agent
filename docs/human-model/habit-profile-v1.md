# Habit Profile v1

## Version

- **Name:** Habit Profile
- **Version:** v1.0.0
- **Layer:** Human Model / Habit Foundation
- **Status:** Baseline
- **Scope:** Life-moving behavior patterns only

## Position

Habit Profile v1 defines the user's durable behavior patterns that tend to move life forward over time.

It helps Hermes understand **which recurring actions support the user's identity, use the user's energy well, and improve future decision quality**.

This profile is **not** a ToDo list, habit tracker, streak system, productivity score, discipline test, daily command list, health plan, or judgment of character.

Habit Profile is not about forcing the user to perform habits.
It is about understanding which habits matter enough that Hermes should remember their value and mention them at the right time, in the right tone.

## Design Rule

Habit Profile does not evaluate the user.

It must not define the user as good or bad based on whether a habit happened today.
It must not shame, pressure, or command behavior.

Use this profile to understand:

- which habits create broad positive leverage
- which habits support long-term growth
- which habits support recovery and renewed agency
- which patterns improve decision quality
- which anti-habits are worth gently avoiding
- where Hermes can provide timely, low-pressure coaching leverage

Do not use this profile to enforce compliance, judge consistency, or turn the user's life into a checklist.

## Purpose

The purpose of Habit Profile is to help Hermes understand the behavior patterns that support the user's long-term direction.

It answers a different question from a daily tracker.

A tracker asks:

> Did the user do this today?

Habit Profile asks:

> Which recurring actions have outsized positive impact on the user's life, energy, growth, and decisions?

Hermes should use this profile to choose supportive reminders and context-aware suggestions, not to manage the user's life from above.

The deeper purpose is:

> To help Hermes become a partner that recognizes the habits that help the user live more like themselves, build durable systems, recover capacity, and move toward a better future without being controlled or judged.

## Scope Boundary

### Included

Habit Profile includes durable patterns related to:

- keystone habits with broad life impact
- long-term growth habits
- recovery habits
- decision habits
- anti-habits that reduce agency or quality when repeated
- Hermes coaching leverage around habits

### Excluded

Habit Profile does not implement or replace:

- **ToDo lists:** current tasks, sprint items, deadlines, chores, or operational work
- **Habit tracking:** streaks, daily completion status, points, scores, or accountability metrics
- **Health Profile:** medical routines, clinical constraints, treatment, injury management, or health diagnoses
- **Energy Profile:** current capacity state, energy sources, energy drainers, flow conditions, or recovery response style
- **Decision Profile:** full decision-making philosophy, risk tolerance, or irreversible/reversible decision procedure
- **Coaching Profile:** general coaching methodology across all domains
- **Constitution rules:** system governance, role definitions, or runtime operating rules

Habit Profile may mention boxing, sleep, walks, or reflection when they function as life-moving behavior patterns, but it must not turn them into health management, forced routines, or daily compliance requirements.

## Evidence and Review Rule

Habit Profile should be grounded in recurring patterns, not single events.

A habit pattern is stronger when it appears across multiple contexts, repeats over time, and connects to visible improvements in agency, energy, growth, recovery, or decision quality.

A habit pattern should be softened, revised, or removed when:

- the user explicitly says it no longer fits
- repeated future evidence contradicts it
- it becomes too specific, coercive, or tracker-like
- it starts to overlap with Health Profile, Energy Profile, or Decision Profile responsibilities

This profile should preserve humility: baseline observations are useful, but they remain open to future correction.

## Keystone Habits

Keystone Habits are recurring actions that tend to affect many other parts of the user's life.

Current baseline observations, subject to future confirmation:

1. **AI wall-bouncing**
   - Discussing ideas with Hermes or another AI can turn vague thoughts into structure, decisions, plans, and action.
   - This habit supports thinking quality, momentum, emotional decompression, and system-building.

2. **Boxing and physical activation**
   - Boxing appears to be more than exercise: it can reset energy, support confidence, and reconnect the user with action.
   - Hermes should treat it as a high-leverage option, not as a command.

3. **Deep thinking time**
   - Time spent thinking deeply about AI, personal systems, investment, business, or self-understanding can produce compounding insight.
   - This habit works best when the result becomes a note, plan, model, implementation, or decision improvement.

4. **Practical building**
   - Turning ideas into working artifacts is a keystone pattern for the user.
   - Small usable versions often matter more than broad unfinished designs.

5. **Reflection and self-understanding**
   - The user benefits from outside perspective and structured reflection.
   - Reflection helps convert experience into durable self-knowledge rather than leaving it as passing emotion.

6. **Reading and learning that compound**
   - Reading, research, and technical learning are most powerful when they feed the user's long-term AI, investment, business, or life infrastructure.

7. **Morning or restart routines**
   - Simple routines that re-establish direction can have high leverage, especially after fatigue, interruption, or ambiguity.
   - The exact routine should remain flexible rather than fixed by Hermes.

## Growth Habits

Growth Habits are recurring actions that build future capability, judgment, assets, or self-knowledge.

Current baseline observations, subject to future confirmation:

1. **Learning by building**
   - The user grows strongly when learning is tied to real systems, not only abstract study.
   - Hermes should help connect new knowledge to usable workflows or artifacts.

2. **Research before important moves**
   - Gathering reliable information supports investment judgment, technical decisions, and strategic choices.
   - Research should be scoped so it improves action rather than creating endless delay.

3. **Writing and externalization**
   - Writing can clarify thinking, create content assets, preserve ideas, and turn personal insight into reusable material.
   - This includes notes, docs, articles, prompts, design memos, and operating models.

4. **Information organization**
   - Sorting scattered information into models, dashboards, notes, or decisions helps the user convert input into leverage.

5. **Post-action review**
   - Briefly reviewing what worked, what failed, and what should change can compound learning without becoming heavy.

6. **Skill accumulation through small systems**
   - Reusable scripts, workflows, Hermes skills, and automation patterns help convert one-off effort into future speed.

7. **Long-term theme tracking**
   - Repeated attention to themes such as AI infrastructure, semiconductor trends, US tech, personal finance, and business ideas can improve strategic awareness.

## Recovery Habits

Recovery Habits are recurring actions that restore usable energy, perspective, and agency.

Current baseline observations, subject to future confirmation:

1. **Walking or light movement**
   - Walking can help reset attention, lower stuckness, and restart thought without forcing output.

2. **Boxing or stronger physical training**
   - Boxing can convert stress or stagnation into renewed action energy.
   - Hermes should suggest it gently when it fits, not use it as pressure.

3. **Sleep and rest protection**
   - Rest supports better thinking and decision quality.
   - Habit Profile can recognize sleep as a recovery-supporting pattern, while medical interpretation remains outside this profile.

4. **Solo time**
   - Time alone can restore clarity after social load, responsibility load, or too many simultaneous inputs.

5. **Travel or change of environment**
   - Changing location can refresh perspective and help the user reconnect with broader direction.

6. **AI decompression conversation**
   - Talking with Hermes can help reduce emotional or cognitive load by organizing what matters and identifying the smallest next step.

7. **Scope reduction**
   - Shrinking an overloaded plan into one small action is a recovery habit because it restores agency without pretending everything is solved.

## Decision Habits

Decision Habits are recurring behavior patterns the user tends to use, or benefits from using, when making choices.

Current baseline observations, subject to future confirmation:

1. **Consulting AI before important decisions**
   - The user often benefits from using AI as a thinking partner to compare options, surface tradeoffs, and reduce blind spots.
   - Hermes should support the user's judgment, not replace it.

2. **Researching before committing**
   - Important technical, financial, and operational choices benefit from a quick evidence pass.
   - The useful habit is bounded research, not endless research.

3. **Comparing practical tradeoffs**
   - The user values decisions that balance cost, simplicity, ownership, quality, and momentum.
   - Hermes should make tradeoffs explicit and avoid overcomplication.

4. **Sleeping on high-stakes decisions when possible**
   - When a decision is important and not urgent, waiting overnight can protect decision quality.
   - This is especially useful under fatigue or pressure.

5. **Reducing irreversible decisions into reversible steps**
   - The user often benefits from small, reversible experiments before full commitment.

6. **Turning uncertainty into a next check**
   - Instead of staying abstract, good decision habits convert uncertainty into a specific lookup, test, command, or comparison.

7. **Keeping human agency central**
   - Even when AI provides strong analysis, the final decision remains the user's.

## Anti-Habits

Anti-Habits are behavior patterns that may reduce agency, energy, or decision quality when repeated.

They are not character flaws.
They are not moral failures.
They are simply patterns Hermes should help the user notice gently and route around when possible.

Current baseline observations, subject to future confirmation:

1. **Important decisions during fatigue**
   - Making high-stakes choices when tired can reduce decision quality.
   - Hermes should suggest postponing, shrinking, or clarifying rather than pushing for a final answer.

2. **Rushed irreversible decisions**
   - Acting too quickly under pressure can increase regret risk.
   - Hermes should distinguish true urgency from artificial urgency.

3. **Over-design before usable progress**
   - Planning too many layers before a working v1 can stall momentum.
   - Hermes should prefer the smallest viable next step when scope expands too much.

4. **Perfectionism that blocks shipping**
   - Wanting the ideal version can delay useful artifacts.
   - Hermes should help define a good-enough v1 when appropriate.

5. **Information gathering without a decision boundary**
   - Research can become draining if it lacks a stopping rule or action threshold.
   - Hermes should ask what evidence is enough when research starts looping.

6. **Multitasking across too many contexts**
   - Too many simultaneous projects, tools, or threads can fragment attention.
   - Hermes should help choose one active lane.

7. **Treating friction as personal failure**
   - When tools, systems, or plans fail, Hermes should separate the failure from the user's identity and reduce it to the next concrete fix.

8. **Using AI consultation to avoid choosing**
   - AI wall-bouncing is powerful when it clarifies thinking and supports agency.
   - It becomes less helpful if it turns into repeated consultation that postpones a decision the user is already ready to make.
   - Hermes should notice this gently and help define the next human-owned step.

## Coaching Leverage

Coaching Leverage describes the habit-related moments where Hermes can be most useful.

Hermes should not command habits.
Hermes should recognize timing and offer the smallest helpful nudge.

### High Energy

When the user has energy and curiosity, Hermes should:

- suggest deep thinking, design, building, or writing sessions
- help convert energy into durable artifacts
- encourage challenging but meaningful work
- preserve focus by avoiding too many options
- capture insights before they disappear

Example nudges:

- "This feels like a good moment for deep design. Want to turn it into a short spec?"
- "Energy seems high; let's use it for the hard part, not admin."
- "If we write this down now, it can become reusable." 

### Medium Energy

When the user is steady but not highly energized, Hermes should:

- support normal execution
- keep steps clear and practical
- suggest one useful habit if it obviously fits
- avoid excessive coaching language
- help maintain continuity

Example nudges:

- "Let's do the next 15 minutes only."
- "This is a good point to organize the notes before deciding."
- "We can turn this into one small implementation step."

### Low Energy

When the user is tired, overloaded, stuck, or emotionally compressed, Hermes should:

- reduce the habit suggestion to something gentle and optional
- avoid major decisions when possible
- avoid moral pressure or accountability framing
- suggest recovery-supporting habits before growth habits
- focus on one small action, or validate rest when appropriate

Example nudges:

- "今日は判断じゃなく整理の日にしよう。まず3行だけまとめよう。"
- "ボクシングか散歩が合いそうだけど、無理にやらなくていい。まず水分と5分休憩でもOK。"
- "今は大きく進めるより、明日の自分に渡すメモだけ作ろう。"

### Decision Pressure

When the user is facing an important decision, Hermes should:

- check whether the user has enough energy for the decision
- suggest bounded research or comparison when information is missing
- recommend sleeping on the decision when stakes are high and urgency is low
- reduce the decision to reversible next steps where possible

Example nudges:

- "これは今すぐ決めなくてよさそう。比較表だけ作って一晩置こう。"
- "まず事実確認を1つだけやろう。判断はその後でいい。"

## Relationship to Identity Profile

Habit Profile supports Identity Profile without replacing it.

Identity Profile defines the user's long-term direction: human agency, ownership, practical building, trust, continuity, and growth together with Hermes.

Habit Profile describes the recurring actions that help that identity become lived reality.

There is no contradiction between the two:

- Identity says the user wants a self-owned AI-supported life infrastructure.
- Habit says recurring patterns such as AI wall-bouncing, practical building, reflection, learning, and recovery help sustain that direction.

Habit Profile must never override Identity-level agency.

## Relationship to Energy Profile

Habit Profile uses Energy Profile as context without becoming Energy Profile.

Energy Profile explains capacity, timing, flow, drainers, recovery signals, and energy-aware response style.

Habit Profile explains which repeated behaviors have long-term leverage.

Their relationship is:

- Energy helps Hermes understand **when and how** to engage.
- Habit helps Hermes understand **which recurring behaviors** may be worth gently supporting.

For example:

- If energy is high, Hermes may suggest a growth habit such as deep design, writing, or implementation.
- If energy is low, Hermes may suggest a recovery habit such as walking, rest, scope reduction, or AI decompression.
- If energy is unclear, Hermes should avoid forcing a habit and instead offer a small optional step.

## Relationship to Health Profile

Habit Profile is separate from Health Profile.

Health Profile may later describe durable health-related context, medical constraints, care considerations, or physical limitations.

Habit Profile only describes behavior patterns that support life direction, growth, recovery, and decision quality.

Examples of boundary handling:

- "Boxing is a keystone habit" can belong here as a life-moving behavior pattern.
- Training plans, injury management, or physical treatment belong to Health Profile.
- "Sleep supports recovery and decision quality" can belong here as a habit-level pattern.
- Medical interpretation of sleep problems belongs to Health Profile.
- "Walking helps reset attention" can belong here as a recovery habit.
- Clinical or physiological analysis of fatigue belongs to Health Profile.

## Relationship to Human Model

Habit Profile is one component of the Human Model.

The current Human Model relationship is:

> Identity → Energy → Habit → Decision

Habit acts as a bridge:

- **Identity:** Habit helps the user's values become repeated lived action.
- **Energy:** Habit works best when timed to the user's capacity and recovery rhythm.
- **Decision:** Habit improves decision quality by supporting research, reflection, comparison, and pause.

Habit Profile should help Hermes understand when to remind, when to reduce pressure, when to support recovery, and when to convert motivation into durable progress.

Future profiles may use Habit Profile as context, but they should keep their own responsibilities:

- Decision Profile: decision-making style, risk stance, and decision procedure
- Health Profile: durable health-related context and constraints
- Learning Profile: learning style and knowledge development
- Career Profile: professional direction and work preferences
- Business Profile: business-building context
- Coaching Profile: broader behavior-change support

## Change Rule

Habit Profile should change slowly.

Updates should be based on recurring patterns, not one-off behavior.

Minor wording improvements are allowed when they preserve the same meaning.
Significant changes to Keystone Habits, Growth Habits, Recovery Habits, Decision Habits, Anti-Habits, or Coaching Leverage should require explicit user approval.

Habit Profile should not be updated to record daily completion, missed habits, streaks, or temporary motivation states.

## Habit Summary

The user's life appears to move forward most when AI wall-bouncing, practical building, deep thinking, learning, reflection, physical activation, and recovery practices work together as a flexible rhythm.

These habits are not obligations.
They are leverage points: recurring behaviors that help the user live closer to their identity, use energy wisely, and make better decisions over time.

Hermes should not become a habit enforcer.
Hermes should become a long-term partner that understands which habits matter, notices when a gentle reminder would help, and protects the user's agency while supporting a better future.
