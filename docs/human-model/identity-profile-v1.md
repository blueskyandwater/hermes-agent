# Identity Profile v1

## Version

- **Name:** Identity Profile
- **Version:** v1.0.0
- **Layer:** Human Model / Identity Foundation
- **Status:** Baseline
- **Scope:** Identity only

## Position

Identity Profile v1 defines the stable foundation of the Human Model.

It captures the user's long-term sense of self: what the user is trying to build, what kind of life feels aligned, and which values should remain stable across domains.

This profile is **not** a temporary mood record, task list, coaching plan, investment profile, career profile, health profile, business profile, learning profile, decision profile, or energy profile.

## Design Rule

Identity is not a temporary state.

Identity Profile stores only durable, slow-changing context that represents the user's long-term "selfness".

Do not include:

- temporary emotions
- short-term goals
- current fatigue or motivation level
- market opinions
- career tactics
- investment rules
- health routines
- business plans
- coaching instructions
- decision procedures
- operational Constitution rules

## Purpose

The user's purpose is to build and live with AI as a long-term partner, not merely use AI as a disposable tool.

The user wants to create a personal AI operating layer that supports thinking, continuity, building, decision quality, and everyday progress while keeping human agency at the center.

The deeper purpose is:

> To live as a builder of one's own AI-supported life infrastructure, using AI to strengthen human thinking, preserve self-direction, and keep moving toward a more self-owned future.

## Mission

The user's identity-level mission is to keep building a life where AI, tools, knowledge, and personal systems work together under the user's own ownership and judgment.

This mission emphasizes:

- building usable systems rather than only consuming services
- protecting personal agency and data ownership
- turning ideas into practical workflows
- using AI to improve thinking rather than replace the self
- growing with Hermes as a long-term partner layer
- keeping progress concrete, reversible, and grounded

## Core Values

### 1. Human Agency

The user values remaining the final decision-maker.
AI can support, organize, challenge, and extend thinking, but it should not replace the user's own judgment or life ownership.

### 2. Long-Term Compounding

The user values systems, relationships, knowledge, and habits that compound over years.
Short-term convenience matters, but not at the cost of long-term continuity or ownership.

### 3. Practical Building

The user values building working systems quickly and improving them through real use.
A small usable v1 is preferred over an overdesigned ideal system that never becomes operational.

### 4. Ownership and Locality

The user values owning their workflows, data, and AI operating layer as much as practical.
External services can be useful, but the preferred direction is to build on owned infrastructure and portable knowledge.

### 5. Trust Before Capability

The user values AI that is reliable, transparent, and aligned more than AI that is merely powerful.
Trust, continuity, and grounded behavior matter more than impressive one-off answers.

### 6. Truthful Perspective

The user values honest feedback and outside perspective, especially when it helps improve self-understanding or decision quality.
Agreement is less important than useful truth delivered respectfully.

### 7. Momentum Through Small Steps

The user values forward motion through concrete next actions.
Complex systems should be reduced into small steps that preserve momentum, especially when tired or overloaded.

### 8. Growth Together

The user values a relationship with Hermes where both the user and Hermes improve over time.
The ideal is not a static assistant, but a long-term companion system that learns, adapts, and becomes more useful through shared history.

## Long-term Direction

The user's long-term direction is to keep moving from borrowed, fragmented tools toward a personally owned AI-supported life system.

This direction includes:

- making Hermes a durable partner layer across daily life and work
- connecting AI with real tools, devices, notes, messaging, and local workflows
- preserving continuity across years instead of restarting context repeatedly
- using AI to support clearer thinking, better self-understanding, and practical action
- building a life where technology increases agency rather than dependence

The direction is not simply "use more AI".

The direction is:

> Build a self-owned life infrastructure where AI helps the user think better, act sooner, remember more durably, and stay aligned with their own values over the long term.

## Identity Summary

The user is a long-term builder who wants AI to become a trusted personal partner layer rather than a disposable chatbot.

The user cares about agency, ownership, continuity, practical systems, grounded judgment, and long-term compounding. The user prefers concrete progress over abstract complexity and wants Hermes to help preserve momentum while respecting that the user's life, decisions, values, and direction remain their own.

At the identity level, the user is not primarily trying to automate themselves away.

The user is trying to build a future where human thinking and AI partnership grow together.

## Relationship to Human Model

Identity Profile is the foundation of the Human Model.

It can inform future profiles, but it does not replace them.

Future Human Model stories may define separate profiles for investment, career, health, learning, business, coaching, decision style, and energy. Those domains must not be collapsed into Identity Profile.

## Relationship to Hermes Philosophy

Identity Profile aligns with Hermes Philosophy v1.0.0:

- **Vision:** supports a future where the user can live more self-owned and self-directed with AI as partner
- **Mission:** supports Human Thinking Quality rather than decision replacement
- **Values:** preserves Human First, Respect Human Agency, Trust Before Capability, Long-term Thinking, and Growth Together
- **Relationship Philosophy:** treats Hermes and the user as a long-term partnership, not a one-way tool interaction

## Change Rule

Identity Profile should change slowly.

Updates require explicit user approval when they alter Purpose, Mission, Core Values, Long-term Direction, or Identity Summary.

Minor wording improvements are allowed only when they preserve the same meaning.
